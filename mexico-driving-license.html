Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to common questions about obtaining a Mexico City driving license.

1. Who can apply for a Mexico City driving license?

Any foreigner with legal residency status in Mexico (temporary or permanent resident) can apply for a Mexico City driving license. You must be at least 18 years old and have a valid residency card (tarjeta de residente).

2. What documents do I need?

Typically required documents include: valid passport, Mexican residency card (temporary or permanent), proof of address in Mexico City (recent utility bill or rental contract), valid foreign driving license (if applicable), CURP (unique population registry code), and proof of blood type. Our team will provide you with a complete checklist and assist with obtaining any missing documents.

3. How long does the process take?

With our assistance, the entire process typically takes between 2 to 4 weeks from initial consultation to receiving your permanent license, depending on appointment availability and document preparation time.

4. Do I need to take a driving test?

Requirements may vary. In many cases, foreigners with valid foreign licenses can exchange or obtain a Mexican license without a practical driving test, though a written theory test may be required. Our team will clarify the specific requirements for your situation.

5. How long is the license valid?

Mexico City permanent driving licenses are typically valid for 3 to 4 years and can be renewed. The exact validity period depends on your age and the license type issued.

6. Can I use my Mexico City license in other states?

Yes, a Mexico City driving license is valid throughout Mexico. You can legally drive in all Mexican states with your CDMX-issued license.

7. What if my documents are not in Spanish?

All foreign language documents must be translated into Spanish by an official translator. We can assist you with arranging certified translations of all required documents.

8. Do I need to live in Mexico City to get this license?

Yes, you need to provide proof of address (comprobante de domicilio) showing residence within Mexico City (CDMX) limits. This can be a utility bill, rental contract, or property deed in your name.

9. What are the costs involved?

Costs include government fees for the license itself, plus our service fees for complete assistance throughout the process. We will provide you with a detailed breakdown of all costs during your initial consultation.

10. Will you accompany me to appointments?

Yes, our service includes accompanying you to all required appointments with Mexico City authorities, handling all communication in Spanish, and ensuring all procedures are completed correctly.

11. What if my application is rejected?

With our thorough preparation and expert knowledge of requirements, rejections are rare. However, if any issues arise, we will work to resolve them and guide you through any additional steps needed.

12. Can I drive while my application is being processed?

If you have a valid foreign license, you may be able to drive during the application process, subject to Mexican regulations. We will advise you on the specific rules applicable to your situation.
